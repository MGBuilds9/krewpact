Executive Summary

What is KrewPact?

KrewPact is a unified platform that replaces the collection of spreadsheets, disconnected tools, and manual processes currently used across MDM Group's five divisions. It combines customer relationship management (CRM), project management, estimating, field operations, and financial tracking into a single system — all connected to ERPNext for accounting and invoicing.

Why Are We Building It?

  • Fragmented tools — sales info in one place, project data in another, estimates in spreadsheets. KrewPact unifies everything.
  • No visibility — managers can't see pipeline, project status, or financials without asking multiple people. KrewPact provides real-time dashboards.
  • Manual handoffs — when a deal closes, someone manually creates the project and re-enters data. KrewPact automates lead → estimate → contract → project flow.
  • Division isolation — each division needs their own data space while executives see across all divisions. KrewPact's role system handles this.

Epic 1: Identity, Access & Organization CORE

The foundation layer — handles who can log in, what they can see, and which division they belong to.

1.1 Authentication (Clerk SSO)

Users sign in with their email or Microsoft 365 account via Clerk. Most MDM staff already have Microsoft accounts in one of four M365 groups: MDM Group, MDM Contracting, MDM Wood, or MDM Telecom. If you have an M365 account, you can sign into KrewPact with one click. For team members without Microsoft accounts (primarily some Telecom field crews), KrewPact supports email + password login as an alternative.

Roles: All users  |  Divisions: All

1.2 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Each user is assigned one of 13 roles (see Roles section above). The system automatically shows/hides features and data based on your role. For example, a field_supervisor sees daily logs and tasks but not financial reports; an executive sees dashboards across all divisions.

Roles: platform_admin manages assignments  |  Divisions: All

1.3 Division Management

MDM Group has 5 divisions, each operating as a semi-independent business unit. KrewPact keeps division data separate — a project manager in MDM Homes only sees Homes data, while an executive or platform_admin can view across all divisions.

Roles: platform_admin, executive (cross-division)  |  Divisions: All 5

Epic 2: CRM & Sales Pipeline CORE

The complete sales engine — from first contact to closed deal. Includes AI-powered lead scoring, automated outreach, and data enrichment.

2.1 Lead Management

Capture new business opportunities as "leads." Each lead tracks the company name, contact info, source (website, referral, cold outreach), and progresses through 6 stages: new → qualified → estimating → proposal_sent → won → lost. Stage history is tracked so you can see exactly when each transition happened.

Roles: project_manager, estimator, operations_manager, executive, platform_admin  |  Divisions: All

2.2 Accounts & Contacts

Maintain a database of companies (accounts) and the people at those companies (contacts). Accounts can be clients, prospects, or trade partners. Each account can have multiple contacts with different roles. Links to leads, opportunities, and projects.

Roles: project_manager, estimator, operations_manager, executive, platform_admin  |  Divisions: All

2.3 Opportunity Pipeline (7 Stages)

When a lead is "won," it converts to an opportunity — a more detailed sales record. Opportunities progress through 7 stages: intake → site_visit → estimating → proposal → negotiation → contracted → closed_lost. Each stage change is logged with timestamps and notes.

Roles: project_manager, estimator, operations_manager, executive  |  Divisions: All

2.4 Activity Logging

Record every interaction with a lead or client — phone calls, emails, meetings, site visits, notes. Activities are linked to the lead/opportunity and visible to the whole sales team so nothing falls through the cracks.

Roles: All internal roles with CRM access  |  Divisions: All

2.5 Lead Scoring (AI-Powered)

Every lead gets an automatic score based on three factors:

  • Fit Score — Does this company match our ideal customer profile? (company size, industry, location)
  • Intent Score — Are they showing buying signals? (engagement, website visits, content downloads)
  • Engagement Score — Are they responding to our outreach? (email opens, replies, meetings booked)

A composite score helps the sales team prioritize which leads to focus on. Higher scores get faster attention.

Roles: Automatic (visible to sales team)  |  Divisions: All

2.6 Outreach Sequences

Automated multi-step outreach campaigns. Set up a sequence like: "Send intro email → wait 3 days → send follow-up → wait 5 days → LinkedIn connection." The system executes each step automatically. Sequences can be paused, edited, or stopped at any time.

Roles: project_manager, estimator, operations_manager  |  Divisions: All

2.7 Enrichment Pipeline

Automatically fills in missing lead data using a "waterfall" of data providers: tries Apollo first, then Clearbit, then LinkedIn, then Google. Finds company size, industry, contact emails, phone numbers, and social profiles without manual research.

Roles: Automatic (background process)  |  Divisions: All

2.8 Dashboard & Reports

Visual dashboards showing: pipeline value by stage, lead sources breakdown, conversion rates, team activity metrics. Filterable by division, date range, and assigned user.

Roles: operations_manager, executive, platform_admin  |  Divisions: All

Epic 7: ERPNext Integration (Core) CORE

Bidirectional sync between KrewPact and ERPNext — the accounting/ERP system. This means data entered in KrewPact automatically appears in ERPNext (and vice versa for some entities), eliminating double data entry.

How it works: When you create or update an account, opportunity, estimate, or project in KrewPact, the change is queued and sent to ERPNext via a background worker (BullMQ). ERPNext handles accounting, invoicing, and financial reporting. KrewPact reads invoices back from ERPNext for display.

Appendix D: Glossary of Terms

TermDefinition
LeadA potential business opportunity — a company or person who might become a client. Enters the pipeline as "new" and progresses through stages.
OpportunityA qualified lead that has been converted into an active sales pursuit. More detailed than a lead, with budget, timeline, and probability.
AccountA company in the system — can be a client, prospect, or trade partner.
ContactA person associated with an account.
EstimateA detailed cost proposal for a project. Becomes a Quotation in ERPNext.
PipelineThe sequence of stages that leads/opportunities move through from first contact to close.
StageA specific step in the pipeline (e.g., "qualified," "proposal_sent," "contracted").
DivisionOne of MDM Group's 5 business units (Contracting, Homes, Wood, Electrical, Telecom).
RBACRole-Based Access Control — the system that determines what each user can see and do based on their role.
ERPNextOpen-source Enterprise Resource Planning system used for accounting, invoicing, and financial management.
BullMQBackground job queue that processes async tasks like ERPNext sync, email sending, and enrichment.
EnrichmentAutomatically filling in missing company/contact data using external data providers (Apollo, Clearbit, LinkedIn).
Lead ScoringAutomatic rating of leads based on fit (company match), intent (buying signals), and engagement (interaction with outreach).
Outreach SequenceA multi-step automated communication campaign (emails, follow-ups) sent to leads over configured time intervals.
RFIRequest for Information — formal question to architect/engineer about project plans or specifications.
SubmittalProduct data, shop drawings, or samples submitted to architect/engineer for approval before installation.
Change OrderA formal modification to the original contract scope, with associated cost and schedule impacts.
Punch ListList of deficiencies or incomplete items to be resolved before a project is considered complete.
Substantial CompletionMilestone when a project is sufficiently complete for its intended use, even if minor items remain.
Trade PartnerA subcontractor or supplier who performs work or provides materials on MDM projects.
RFQRequest for Quotation — invitation sent to trade partners to bid on a specific scope of work.
ClerkAuthentication service that handles user sign-in, SSO, and session management.
SupabaseDatabase platform (PostgreSQL) with built-in row-level security, storage, and real-time features.
